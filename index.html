Peer is the engine that does — agent-accelerated, but never agent-only. Fourteen specialist agents. Human verification on every assessment, with 10% blind-graded a second time. EU-hosted infrastructure. Load-bearing capability behind every engagement.

Mastery decisions are agent-accelerated, never agent-only.

Founder · Greg McCallum

Mastery Engineering exists because I needed it to.

The No BS GTM Advisor

I've spent fifteen years in commercial seats — starting as a cold caller, up through partnership and enterprise sales, then the seats that lead them: sales leader, Head of Sales, VP, CCO, fractional GTM advisor — across high-growth companies on both sides of the Atlantic. The seats where the question "is enablement actually working?" stops being abstract and starts being your performance review.

In every one of those seats, I watched the same pattern. Enablement vendors sold methodologies. Managers coached opinions. Output platforms scored deals. And underneath all of it, the layer that actually decided whether reps ramped — the Mastery Layer — went unobserved. Reps were diagnosed in language no one could verify and coached on plays they couldn't yet execute.

The frameworks started as my own — built one at a time, out of necessity, from cold calls up, because I needed them to get better. I formalised them into The Mastery Standard because the diagnoses needed a rubric. I built Peer because the rubric needed something to operate it. I built ThinkWork because the standard and the engine were useless without someone walking in and installing them. Mastery Engineering is what this work is called when it's done right.
